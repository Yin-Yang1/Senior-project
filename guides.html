Safety Guides

Learn about common cyber threats and how to protect yourself.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a scam where criminals try to trick you into giving them sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers by pretending to be a trustworthy entity.

How to Avoid Phishing:

  1. Don't click on links in unexpected emails.
  2. Verify the sender's email address.
  3. Look for spelling errors or urgent language.
  4. Use antivirus software with phishing protection.

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts your files and demands payment to unlock them.

How to Protect Against Ransomware:

  1. Keep your software and operating system updated.
  2. Backup your important files regularly.
  3. Don't open attachments from unknown sources.
  4. Use reputable antivirus software.

Unsafe Wi-Fi Networks

Public Wi-Fi can be risky because hackers might intercept your data.

Safe Wi-Fi Practices:

  1. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities.
  2. Use a VPN when on public networks.
  3. Ensure websites use HTTPS (look for the lock icon).
  4. Turn off file sharing when on public Wi-Fi.
